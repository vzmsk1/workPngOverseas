Managed Pressure Drilling

PNG OVERSEAS is dedicated to technological advancement and is constantly on the lookout for the cutting-edge technologies that offer ways to make drilling safer for both the environment and the drilling crew, faster, less costly as well as overall more efficient.

Why MPD

  • PNG OVERSEAS utilizes Mud Pressure Drilling technology as opposed to the more traditional approaches in order to manage risks that come with well drilling. Application of the MPD-UBD technology enables to essentially mitigate risks of well control loss in the course of drilling and the related therewith secondary risks. At the same time, the said technologies are cheaper than traditional solutions and techniques applied, making it a more attractive feature to the Client.
  • The use of this technology is recommended when drilling in the so-called "narrow window zones". In such conditions, insignificant variations of mud density may cause either mud loss or oil/gas/water kicks.
  • MPD is also used in situations when it is difficult to forecast the presence and/or characteristics of narrow drilling window zones because of insufficient geological data of drilling concession area.

Traditional way

  • In situations of narrow drilling window zones and insufficient geological data the first thing to do is to try to change mud weight/density (reducing weight or weighting up) in order to balance pore pressure and well column mud pressure.
  • However, this requires additional time and, as experience has proven, sometimes it is too late to take such measures. Then you have to stop drilling and deal with complications. There is also a high risk of serious accidents.

PNG OVERSEAS way

  • The offered MPD technique uses dedicated sensors enabling almost real time monitoring of mud density, temperature and flow rate in and out. This data can be provided to contractor in on-line mode. Contractor is informed of loss or kicks in well within less than one minute from the corresponding event occurrence.
  • Dedicated electronic adjustable wellhead choke (located downstream of rotating BOP) enables to adjust outlet well choke (from 0 to 100% opening). Therefore, it is possible to immediately change bottom hole pressure by means of fully opened inlet valve (delivery line manifold), on the one hand, and partly closed by choke regulator outlet valve, on the other hand. The difference between mud flow rate in and out creates fluid column adjustable hydrodynamic pressure onto open bore hole and onto bottom hole.
